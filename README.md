# Math is Beautiful

A growing tribute to the beauty of mathematics, live at [math.learnerd.in](https://math.learnerd.in)

## What this is

An interactive museum, not a textbook. Six exhibits where you touch the math and the math responds. Built on one belief: nobody hates math, people hate how math was handed to them.

Every exhibit is live code. There are no images anywhere on this site. Every spiral, every curve, every pattern is an equation drawing itself on your screen, right now.

## The exhibits

**01 · The Golden Spiral (φ)**
Drag a slider and watch the spiral grow, square by square, the way nature builds it. Overlay a sunflower and see 500 seeds pack themselves at the golden angle.

**02 · The Circle, Unrolled (π)**
Roll a circle along a line and watch its edge lay flat. It stops at exactly 3.14159 diameters. Every circle, every size, everywhere in the universe. Then watch pi's infinite digits become a walk that never repeats.

**03 · The Primes Whisper (ℙ)**
The Ulam spiral. Primes look like chaos until you plot them in a spiral, then diagonal lines appear that mathematicians still cannot fully explain. An open mystery, on your screen.

**04 · Draw, and Math Draws Back (∿)**
Sketch anything with your finger. A chain of spinning circles redraws it perfectly, using a real Fourier transform. Slide the circle count down and watch your drawing blur like a fading memory. This is how JPEG, MP3 and your phone calls work.

**05 · Ramanujan's Taxicab (1729)**
London, 1918. Hardy calls his taxi number dull. Ramanujan, from his sickbed, instantly: it is the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two different ways. Watch 1,729 cubes build themselves both ways, then hunt for taxicab numbers yourself.

**06 · Drawn by Equations (✿)**
The design wing. Five plates where every artist is a formula: rose curves, Maurer roses, Lissajous figures, phyllotaxis, and a Victorian harmonograph. Every parameter is a slider. You are the co-designer.

## Why it exists

I became a designer because I loved math, not despite it. This museum is my attempt to pass that luck forward, one exhibit at a time.

It is a tribute, and tributes are never finished. Every time math makes me stop, a new plate joins the collection.

## Tech

One HTML file. No frameworks, no build step, no dependencies, no images. HTML, CSS and vanilla JavaScript on canvas. Deployed on Vercel, updated through this repo.

Fonts: Fraunces, Cormorant Garamond, IBM Plex Mono.

## Running locally

Download `index.html` and open it in a browser. That is the entire setup.
